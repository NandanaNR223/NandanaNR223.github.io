About

I am a linguist first — gold medallist, fieldworker, close reader of language in all its forms. My M.S. from the University at Buffalo and my years working with aphasia corpora, TTS systems, and AI bias evaluation have given me something rare: the ability to see language the way both a humanist and a data practitioner would. I am natively fluent in Malayalam and Tamil, professionally fluent in English and Hindi, and deeply curious about what happens when machines try to do what humans do with words.

Projects

Project 001 — Featured Tool

Referential Density Analyzer

An interactive tool that measures referential density in any text — the proportion of meaning carried by pronouns, demonstratives, and substitutions versus explicit lexical content. Built on Halliday & Hasan's cohesion framework, with direct connections to clinical patterns observed in aphasic discourse. Paste any text and see its referential structure annotated in real time.

Original fieldwork analyzing Malayalam–Tamil code-switching among 20-30 bilingual speakers in Coimbatore. Collected and curated conversational datasets, investigated discourse-level patterns in language alternation, and examined the social and pragmatic motivations behind multilingual switching.
